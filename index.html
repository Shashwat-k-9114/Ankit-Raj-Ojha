Note on the Author

About

ARO

Ankit was born and raised in Chapra, Bihar, India. An engineering graduate, he worked as a software engineer with Infosys Limited before doing a master's degree in English, and a PhD in English from IIT Roorkee.

Ankit is currently based in Karnal where he works as an assistant professor of English with the Department of Higher Education, Haryana. His poetry, short fiction, non-fiction and research articles have appeared in journals, magazines and anthologies based in twenty countries.

Ankit is the author of Pinpricks (2022) and editor of Wives (2023) and The Bare Bones Book of Humour (2026). He is also a founding editor of the EBSCO-indexed magazine, The Hooghly Review, has guest-edited a volume of Essence & Critique: Journal of Literature and Drama Studies, and is a consulting editor with several Routledge and Springer Nature journals.

Ankit won the 2023 Briefly Think Essay Prize, was a finalist in the Sundress 2023 Broadside Contest, and made the top 1% in the Wergle Flomp Humour Poetry Contest 2023.

For "How to tame your dragons," published in Porcupine Literary. A poem he still doesn't fully understand winning any attention, which might be why it did.

An international contest for deliberately bad poetry. He entered on a dare, more or less, and ranked embarrassingly well. Lists it here next to the Pushcart on purpose.
